## Key Design Decisions

### 1. **Temporal Snapshots** (Not cross-sectional)
- **Why**: Prevents label leakage, creates time-aware training data
- **Trade-off**: More data preprocessing, but production-realistic

### 2. **False Positive Penalty Economics** (Not simple cost)
- **Why**: Targeting non-churners is expensive (wasted discount + gaming)
- **Trade-off**: More complex EV formula, but realistic profit curves

### 3. **Budget Gate in Decision Engine** (Not just rank-and-take-top-N)
- **Why**: Production systems have real constraints
- **Trade-off**: Targets fewer customers, but respects operational limits

### 4. **Unconstrained Mode Separate** (Not just one optimization)
- **Why**: Strategic planning needs to know the "true optimum" unconstrained
- **Trade-off**: Two code paths, but clean separation of concerns

### 5. **Monthly Batch** (Not real-time streaming)
- **Why**: Churn is slow-moving, campaigns take weeks to execute
- **Trade-off**: Less "real-time," but much lower infrastructure cost

## Scalability Considerations

**Current Scale** (7k customers):
- Feature engineering: ~3 seconds
- Model training: ~4 seconds
- Batch inference: ~1 second
- Total pipeline: <15 seconds

**Production Scale** (1M customers):
- Feature engineering: Spark/Dask (distributed)
- Model training: Incremental learning or sampling
- Batch inference: Partitioned scoring (100k batches)
- Feature store: BigTable or Redis (online)
- Estimated runtime: ~30 minutes

**Bottlenecks to Address**:
1. Rolling features (computationally expensive) â†’ Pre-aggregate
